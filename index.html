How to Add Your Signature in Gmail

Step 1: On a computer, click Copy Signature above. The first time, your browser may ask for permission to copy. Click Allow.

Step 2: Add it to Gmail:

  1. Open Gmail and click the Settings gear icon (top right), then See all settings
  2. Under General, scroll to Signature
  3. Click Create new, give it a name (for example, "Kellenberg"), and paste into the signature box (Ctrl+V or Cmd+V)
  4. Under Signature defaults, choose your new signature for new emails and for replies/forwards
  5. Scroll to the bottom and click Save Changes

Common Questions

Should I set this up on my computer or my phone?

On a computer. The full signature, with the school logo, only works when you set it up on a computer. Do the steps above one time on a computer and every email you send from that computer will have it.

Will this signature show up on emails I send from my iPhone?

Not on its own. The Mail app on your iPhone keeps its own signature, separate from the one above, and it can only hold plain text. That means your name and title will show, but not the logo or the colors. To set a simple one, open the iPhone Settings app, tap Apps, then Mail, then Signature (on an older iPhone it is simply Settings, then Mail, then Signature). Type a few lines such as your name, your title, and Kellenberg Memorial.

I clicked Copy Signature and nothing seemed to happen.

It most likely worked anyway. Go to Gmail and paste to check before trying again. If your browser asked for permission, be sure you clicked Allow. If it still will not copy, click open this page in its own tab and copy from there.

When I pasted, the logo and colors were gone.

That happens when the signature is pasted on a phone, or into the wrong box. Set it up on a computer, in the Gmail signature box you reach through Settings, then See all settings, then General, then Signature. Paste with the keyboard: hold Ctrl and press V on Windows, or hold Command and press V on a Mac.

My title, extension, or email changed. Do I have to redo it?

Yes, and it only takes a minute. Come back to this page, type in your new information, click Copy Signature, and paste it over your old signature in Gmail using the same steps. Click Save Changes when you finish.

It still is not showing up on my emails.

Check two things in the Gmail signature settings. First, under Signature defaults, choose your Kellenberg signature for new emails and for replies. Second, scroll to the very bottom of the settings page and click Save Changes.
